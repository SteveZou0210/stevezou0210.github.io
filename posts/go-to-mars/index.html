到火星去

技术主义和人文主义的交锋注定是我思考一生的课题。

一方面，在最喜爱书籍里毅然决然放上阿西莫夫的我，自然对辉煌的太空歌剧情有独钟，

“人类的赞歌就是勇气的赞歌”

星空一向都是能让我心潮澎湃的心向之地，星辰大海才应该是人类的归宿；

但另一方面，正如 Sandel 暗示的那样，如果抱着到火星去就能解决一切问题的心态，事情恐怕会变得更糟。

“The very idea that going to the moon or to Mars is kind of a natural outgrowth of this way of thinking(Silicon Valley Techno-Utopianism), where they can start a new–it’s an ambition really to start from scratch with a clean slate, to begin a new unfettered uncomplicated by disagreement, contestation, challenge, dialogue, controversy where they can start a new and build a society from scratch that is not as they might see it, weighted down by the controversies that arise whenever human beings gather in society–it’s fantastical, but it’s also dangerous and hubristic, because it implies they are the avatars, they are the pioneers, they are the initiators of this new frontier that breaks free from the messy to negotiate, to discuss, to disagree which is essential to the human experience.”

– Michael J. Sandel

建设性的工作困难并值得尊敬；权衡、讨论、思辨的工作必不可少——这两句话说出来轻松，但是要在哪一句前加上“但是”一词呢？

上至人类集体，下至我自己，这种斗争似乎必然存在。

Steve Zou
